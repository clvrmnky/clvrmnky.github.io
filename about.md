# About

I've been in design for 25 years. The last ten or so have been almost entirely in health tech: cardiac diagnostics, medication delivery, ambient clinical documentation, and now clinical AI for hospital revenue cycle.

I spent close to three years at AliveCor, starting as a product design lead and ending up Director, Head of UX. Most of that time went into standing up a UX practice from scratch, including building out a design team in Bangalore, and redesigning the Kardia consumer app. After that I ran UX for medication delivery systems at BD, then spent a stretch running my own studio and taking on independent projects.

In 2024 I joined Freed, working on AI that listens to patient visits and drafts the clinical note automatically. Since early 2025 I've led UX & Product Design at SmarterDx, where the work spans the clinical interface, the reasoning layer that explains what the AI found and why, and the evaluation framework that checks whether those explanations actually hold up. I also built Component Lab, a code-first design system that replaced Figma as source of truth for the design team.

Outside of work I build things because the tool I wanted didn't exist: a knowledge graph system with 54K extracted entities, a persistent memory architecture for AI-assisted work, a precision genomics dashboard modeling my own biological data.

AI is only as good as what it's given and how its output is designed. I've spent my career on both sides of that equation, first the interface, now increasingly what's underneath it.
