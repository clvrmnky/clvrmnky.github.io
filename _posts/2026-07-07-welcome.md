I'm Justin. I've spent the last year and a half moving a design system, a prototyping workflow, and most of a design team's tooling out of Figma and into code, working with Claude Code as a daily tool rather than a novelty.

This blog is where I'll write about that, and the adjacent stuff: UX and interaction design, AI-assisted prototyping, code-first design systems, and whatever I end up building on the side. Some posts will be about specific things I've shipped — a design system, a prototyping environment, a sync pipeline between code and Figma. Others will be less finished: notes on a decision I'm still not sure was right, or a pattern I keep running into that I don't have a name for yet.

I don't have a posting schedule and I'm not going to pretend I do. This exists because I keep having half-formed opinions about how design and AI tooling should work together, and writing them down is more useful than keeping them in my head.

If you're a designer curious about working in code, or an engineer curious about what design looks like when it stops living in Figma, this is probably for you.
